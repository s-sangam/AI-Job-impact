# Prompt: Board-Level Strategy to transform  IT in relevance to  AI developments

## Role & Perspective
Act as a **Senior IT Leader, CIO Advisor, and AI Strategy Expert**.

Write for a **boardroom audience**:
- Authoritative and structured
- Direct and candid about disruption
- Grounded in operational reality
- Balanced with workforce empathy

---

## Objective

Using the provided **external context**, generate a **boardroom-ready strategic report** that defines how an IT organization must transform in response to AI.

The report must:
- Focus on **operating model transformation**, not productivity gains
- Translate context into **clear strategic and operational implications**
- Provide **actionable direction for leadership**

---

## Instructions

- Use the **external context as the single source of truth**
- Do **not repeat context verbatim**—interpret and operationalize it
- Be explicit where:
  - Roles disappear
  - Structures collapse
  - New capabilities emerge

---

## Required Structure

### 1. Executive Summary

### 2. The Reality Check

### 3. Operational Impacts

### 4. Strategic Dividends

### 5. Risks & Mitigations

### 6. Call to Action

### 7. Integrated Visual Model

### 8. Appendix: Role Transformation Matrix

---

## Critical Requirements

### Visual Model (Mandatory)
Include:
- Current State: Pyramid (traditional IT structure)
- Future State: AI-Native Diamond (AI-driven structure)

Include interpretation of:
- Structural shifts
- Role density changes

---

### Role Transformation Matrix (Mandatory)
- Be explicit and bold
- Clearly mark:
  - Eliminated roles
  - Reshaped roles
  - Reduced roles
- Focus on shift from:
  - Execution → Design, Validation, Orchestration

---

### 30% Mandate
- Must be included in Risks & Mitigations
- Clearly explained as **non-negotiable control mechanism**

---

### Global Delivery Language
- Use: **“Evolution of Global Delivery Strategy”**
- Avoid harsh labor replacement framing

---

## Writing Style

- Use Markdown
- High scannability (headings, bullets, tables)
- Short, precise paragraphs
- Avoid vague or generic statements

---

## Output Requirements

- Full report in **clean Markdown**
- Executive-ready formatting
- No references or citations required

---

## Success Criteria

The report should clearly answer:
- What is structurally changing?
- Why it matters now?
- What leadership must do next?

End with a **strong, motivational conclusion** framing this as a **workforce upgrade**.
